Start fuelling 30–40 minutes into the ride and aim for 40–60 g carbs per hour.

  • Porridge with honey
  • White toast + jam
  • Bagel + honey
  • Rice Krispies + banana

First 60–90 Minutes

  • Begin fuelling after 30–40 minutes
  • Jelly babies, wine gums, cereal bars, carb drink

Coffee Stop

  • 1–2 bars or a pastry
  • Flat white or sugary drink

Mid-Ride

  • 40–60 g carbs/hr (mix of sweets, bars, carb drink)

Lunch Stop

  • White-bread sandwiches
  • Coke or lemonade

Choose low‑fibre, low‑fat, high‑carb foods 2–2.5 hours before riding.

Best Options

  • Porridge (white oats) + honey
  • White toast + jam
  • Bagel + honey
  • Rice Krispies + banana

Avoid Before Riding

  • Soaked oats
  • Chia seeds, flax seeds, pumpkin seeds
  • Wholemeal bread
  • Heavy dairy
  • Peanut butter

Testing & Progress Tracking

The Kolie Moore Baseline Test is a steady-state FTP test that reflects real-world performance far better than a ramp test. It measures your true sustainable power, fatigue resistance, and pacing ability.

Why Use This Test?

  • More accurate than ramp tests for trained riders
  • Reflects real climbing and endurance performance
  • Less dependent on VO2max “ceiling”
  • More repeatable and less noisy
  • Gives insight into durability, not just a single number

When to Use It

  • Every 8–10 weeks
  • Only if FTP has changed by more than 10%
  • After a recovery week (e.g., Week 8)

How the Test Works

The Baseline Test is simple:

  1. 10–15 min warm-up (include a few short efforts)
  2. Start a long steady effort at your best guess of FTP
  3. Hold that power as steadily as possible
  4. Increase slightly in the final 3–5 minutes if you can
  5. Stop when you can no longer maintain the target power

Your FTP is the average power of the entire steady-state effort.

Pacing Tips

  • Start at a power you could hold for 40–50 minutes
  • If you feel good at 10 minutes, hold steady
  • If you feel good at 15 minutes, add 2–5 watts
  • If you feel good at 20 minutes, add another 2–5 watts
  • Finish strong but controlled

What a Good Test Looks Like

  • Power is steady with a slight rise at the end
  • Heart rate rises gradually (cardiac drift)
  • You finish with nothing left but without blowing up early

Common Mistakes

  • Starting too hard (most common)
  • Using it like a 20-minute TT — it’s not
  • Spiking power on climbs or accelerations
  • Testing too often (stick to every 8–10 weeks)

How It Fits Into Your Rolling Plan

  • Placed in Week 8 of each block
  • Thursday climbs reduced to Z2–Z3 if testing
  • FTP only updated if change is >10%
  • Plan continues unchanged if FTP is stable

This test gives you a realistic, stable FTP that matches how you actually ride outdoors.
